UX Internship

About


Warwick University is a young publicly funded research university located in Coventry, England. Every year over the summer, the university runs an intership program for Warwick undergraduates.


I started studying at Warwick in October 2012 for a Masters in Computer Science. In the summer of 2015, I did an internship in IT Services as part of Warwick's Undergraduate Internship Program. After my internship, I stayed on part time as a Junior UX until September 2016. During my time at Warwick, I learned many transferable skills.

User Testing


Test observer: Initially, I observed tests, from both inside and outside the testing room. Outside of the testing room I answered questions from the stakeholders in the room with me regarding what we were watching. In real time, I explained to them the techniques we were using, and how they were bringing out certain reactions in the participants. In the testing room, I wrote notes on my observations, and interjected with questions whenever appropriate.

Test facilitator: I was given the responsibility to conduct tests independently, acting as the primary facilitator. I guided users through the tasks written for them, prompting them to be vocal when needed and encouraging them to be honest about the system in front of them. It was also my job to make sure that the user's reactions were natural, so it was important for me to hold back on guiding them too heavily, as well as fishing for certain responses, to avoid results bias.

Reports


I wrote a detailed report on the methods I used in testing, a numerical analysis of test observations, and concluded with justified suggestions for change.

Programs used: Microsoft Office

Video editing


All of the footage from user tests were edited into short films that highlighted key comments. Editing also included adding screen annotations and relevant callouts. We gatheread several hours of footage, so condensing each user group into a video that only lasted a few minutes was important. Annotations were also added on screen to aid those unfamiliar with the testing schedule.

Programs used: Camtasia, Morae

Presentation


After the internship, I was asked to present findings to stakeholders. This presentation included brief details on how tests were conducted, as well as some of the analysis from the report. I was expected to answer any questions about the testing, and to relay key changes needed to the developers.

Programs used: Microsoft Office
